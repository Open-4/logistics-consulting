## Heavy Equipment Transport

Project logistics for heavy equipment requires specialized solutions:

**Breakbulk vessels:** For oversized/heavy items, loaded by crane or roll-on/roll-off. Can handle single pieces weighing hundreds of tons.

**Special containers:** Open-top (high cargo), flat rack (wide), platform (long).

**SPMT (Self-Propelled Modular Transporters):** Can load equipment at the factory, roll onto vessel, and deliver directly to site, minimizing handling.

## Engineering Logistics Challenges

Engineering logistics differs from standard freight: each project is unique (low standardization), involves multimodal transport across multiple countries, and has tight deadlines where delays impact construction schedules.

**Common issues:** Poor destination infrastructure, slow customs in project countries, changing local regulations, and critical time sensitivity.

## OOG Cargo Considerations

OOG (Out of Gauge) cargo exceeds standard container dimensions. Items over 2.4m height, 2.4m width, or 12m length typically need special arrangements.

**Key steps:** Book early (3-5 days longer lead time), budget for extras (OOG surcharge, special equipment fees), verify route clearances (factory doors, port gates, bridge heights), prepare detailed cargo drawings, and secure comprehensive insurance.
