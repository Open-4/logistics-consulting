## HS Code Classification Basics

HS codes are standardized product classification numbers used by customs authorities worldwide. Most countries use a 6-digit base, with additional digits for national requirements (US: 10, China: 13, EU: 8).

**Classification principles:** Start with the specific heading, consider the product''s material composition and function, and check for any special rules.

**Why it matters:** Incorrect classification can lead to overpaying duties, customs delays, fines, or even legal consequences.

## Tariff Differences by Country

**US:** 0-37.5% duty rates. Section 301 tariffs apply to ~$550B of Chinese goods.

**EU:** Average rate ~3.5% but varies widely (clothing 12%, electronics 0%).

**ASEAN:** Preferential rates under China-ASEAN FTA for most goods.

**Japan:** Average ~4.2% with low industrial goods tariffs.

## Required Clearance Documents

**Basic:** Commercial invoice, packing list, bill of lading/AWB, certificate of origin

**Special:** Health certificates (food), MSDS (chemicals), product certifications (CCC, CE, FDA), import/export licenses (controlled goods)
