## Background

In April 2026, the General Administration of Customs of China jointly issued new cross-border e-commerce retail export regulations, effective July 1, 2026. These represent the most significant policy adjustment since the 2020 B2B export pilot program, aimed at standardizing export procedures, improving customs efficiency, and strengthening IP protection.

## Key Changes

### 1. Enhanced Declaration Requirements

Standardized product descriptions (brand, model, material, use), 6+ digit HS codes, and transparent pricing information including actual transaction amounts and shipping costs are now required to combat undervaluation.

### 2. End-to-End Tracking

Full shipment traceability from dispatch to delivery, real-time status updates to customs systems, and tracking integration for returned goods.

### 3. Streamlined Processes for Compliant Firms

Green channel access for advanced certification enterprises, expanded "export returns center warehouse" model, and broader "inspect before shipment" pilot programs.

## Benefited Sectors

Compliant large-scale sellers, overseas warehouse operators, and digital service providers (ERP/WMS/customs system upgrades).

## Recommendations

1. Upgrade information management systems
2. Build standardized product databases
3. Complete customs system API integration
4. Conduct team training
5. Assess compliance cost impact on pricing
