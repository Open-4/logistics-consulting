## Trend 1: Nearshoring & Supply Chain Restructuring

According to McKinsey''s 2025 supply chain survey, over 65% of global manufacturers have initiated or plan to relocate production capacity to nearshoring destinations including Mexico, Vietnam, India, and Eastern Europe within two years. This "China+1" strategy has evolved into more diversified regional layouts, with intra-Asian trade growing while transpacific volumes moderate.

## Trend 2: Accelerated Digital Logistics

The global digital logistics market reached USD 58 billion in 2025, with 18%+ CAGR projected through 2030 (Transport Intelligence). Key drivers include: freight management platforms becoming industry standard, IoT for real-time cargo monitoring, and blockchain for trade finance and e-BL.

## Trend 3: Green Logistics & ESG

The EU CBAM entered its implementation transition phase in January 2026. According to DHL''s 2026 Logistics Trend Radar, 73% of logistics companies now list sustainability as a core strategic objective. Key initiatives include modal shift (air to sea/rail), new energy fleets, and carbon footprint visibility.

## Trend 4: Automation & AI

Gartner predicts that by 2027, over 40% of large logistics enterprises will deploy AI-driven supply chain decision systems. In 2026, key applications include: AI-powered demand forecasting (85-95% accuracy), warehouse automation (2-3x picking efficiency with AGV/AMR), and intelligent transport scheduling (10-15% cost reduction).

## Trend 5: Labor Shortages

The global freight forwarding industry faces a 15% talent gap (FIATA 2025 data). Warehouse operators and truck driver shortages are accelerating automation adoption while pushing up logistics labor costs by an estimated 5-8% in 2026.
