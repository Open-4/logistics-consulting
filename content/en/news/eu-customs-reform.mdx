## Background

The EU Customs Reform Package, adopted in 2025 and effective March 1, 2028, represents the largest customs system overhaul since the Customs Union was established in 1968. The phased implementation runs through 2032, with the core objective of digitalizing customs processes, improving risk management efficiency, and strengthening e-commerce import controls.

## Key Changes

### 1. EU Customs Data Hub

A unified Data Hub replacing 27 separate national systems: single-window submission, AI-powered risk analysis, and a data-first approach requiring full digital declarations 48 hours before cargo arrival.

### 2. De Minimis Elimination

The current sub-EUR 150 duty-free threshold for low-value imports will be eliminated. All commercial imports will require customs declarations, impacting approximately 65% of e-commerce parcel imports.

### 3. Simplified Clearance for Compliant Firms

AEO-certified and high-compliance importers will benefit from reduced data requirements, lower intervention rates, and faster clearance times.

## Impact on Chinese Exporters

- 5-15 EUR per parcel cost increase for e-commerce sellers
- Enhanced digital compliance requirements (ERP/customs data management)
- Increased value of AEO certification

## Recommendations

1. Upgrade digital systems for EU Data Hub interface
2. Assess cost impact for e-commerce operations
3. Pursue AEO certification before 2028
4. Monitor phased implementation timeline
