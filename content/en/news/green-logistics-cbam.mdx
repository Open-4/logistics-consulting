## CBAM Status

The EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) entered its implementation phase in January 2026, with a transition period through end-2026. From January 2027, importers will need to purchase CBAM certificates, marking the world''s first carbon tariff regime. The World Bank estimates CBAM will impose approximately USD 2-3.5 billion in annual carbon costs on Chinese exports to the EU.

## Covered Products

Steel (largest impact, ~EUR 15 billion annual exports), aluminum, cement, fertilizers, electricity, and hydrogen.

## Logistics Impact

- Maritime and air freight emissions included in CBAM product carbon footprint calculations
- Low-carbon modes (sea, rail) gain cost advantage over high-carbon modes (air)
- Full-chain carbon data collection and reporting required

## Action Roadmap

### Short-term (2026)
- Carbon baseline assessment for key export products
- Carbon data management system establishment
- CBAM compliance training

### Medium-term (2027-2028)
- Modal shift optimization (increase sea/rail share)
- Green logistics partner selection
- Supply chain carbon management integration

### Long-term (2029+)
- Renewable energy transition in production
- International carbon neutrality certification
- Carbon trading market participation
