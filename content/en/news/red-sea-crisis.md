## Background

Since early 2025, deteriorating security in the Red Sea-Suez Canal corridor has forced major carriers to suspend Red Sea routes and divert via the Cape of Good Hope. The Suez Canal handles approximately 12% of global trade and 30% of container traffic, making this crisis comparable in scale to the 2021 Ever Given blockage.

## Cost Analysis

The Cape route adds 8,000-10,000 km and 10-14 days compared to the Suez route. Associated costs include: USD 300,000-400,000 additional fuel per 10,000 TEU vessel, approximately 25% reduction in vessel turnaround efficiency, war risk premiums surging from 0.05% to 0.5-1.0% of cargo value, and roughly USD 500,000-700,000 saved in transit fees (insufficient to offset operational cost increases).

## Rate Impact (SSEFC Data)

- China-North Europe: 150-200% increase vs. pre-crisis
- China-Mediterranean: 200-250% increase
- China-US East Coast: 60-80% indirect increase

## Impact on Chinese Exports

- Significant freight cost increases for Europe/Mediterranean exports
- 10-14 day longer delivery lead times requiring inventory adjustments
- Capacity shortages during peak seasons

## Recommendations

1. Lock in contract rates with carriers
2. Consider China-Europe Railway Express alternatives
3. Build safety stock near European ports
4. Establish flexible contingency mechanisms
