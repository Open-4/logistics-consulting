## Trend 1: Supply Chain Resilience Becomes Mandatory

Frequent geopolitical conflicts, extreme weather events, and public health emergencies have made supply chain resilience a business imperative rather than a strategic option. In 2026, we see more enterprises transitioning from lean, efficiency-maximizing supply chains to resilient ones through multi-sourcing, safety stock increases, and regionalized network design.

## Trend 2: Green Logistics Moves from Pledges to Action

With CBAM implementation and tightening carbon regulations worldwide, green logistics has moved from CSR commitments to operational requirements:

- **Modal Shift**: Increased adoption of ocean and rail transport, reducing air freight share
- **New Energy Fleets**: Accelerated deployment of electric and hydrogen fuel cell trucks for mid-range transport
- **Carbon Transparency**: Growing demand for full lifecycle carbon footprint disclosure

## Trend 3: AI Deeply Reshapes Logistics Operations

AI is moving from proof-of-concept to large-scale deployment. Key applications in 2026 include:

- **Intelligent Forecasting**: AI-powered demand forecasting achieving 85%-95% accuracy
- **Dynamic Scheduling**: Real-time route optimization based on traffic and order changes
- **Automated Documentation**: OCR + NLP applications in bill of lading and customs documentation

## Trend 4: China-Europe Railway Express Network Expansion

The China-Europe Railway Express continues rapid growth, adding new routes to more European inland cities. Meanwhile, the Southern Corridor (China-Southeast Asia) and Arctic routes are accelerating commercial exploration.

## Trend 5: Cross-border E-commerce Logistics Enters Precision Operations Era

As market growth moderates, cross-border e-commerce logistics is shifting focus to cost control, delivery reliability, and returns experience. Overseas warehouse networks and intelligent routing systems have become core competitive differentiators.
