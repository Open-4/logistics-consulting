## Background

Our client, a major international EPC contractor, was constructing a large thermal power plant project in Southeast Asia. Critical equipment including generator stators, transformers, and boiler components had single-piece weights up to 150 tons and lengths exceeding 25 meters. These oversized items needed transport from multiple manufacturing bases in China via ocean, river barge, and overland routes to a remote construction site—a complex, multi-stage logistics chain with significant risk.

## Challenges

- **Oversized & Heavy Cargo**: Generator stator at 150 tons far exceeded standard container capacity, requiring specialized transport equipment and lifting plans
- **Complex Multi-Stage Route**: Factory pickup → domestic trucking → port loading → ocean shipping → port discharge → river barge → site delivery across 3 countries and 5 operational nodes
- **Poor Site Infrastructure**: Remote project location with narrow access roads and insufficient bridge capacity requiring temporary reinforcement
- **Tight Timeline**: No schedule flexibility; delays would incur tens of thousands of dollars in daily liquidated damages

## Solution

### Route Survey & Planning

The project team conducted three on-site route surveys starting three months before transport. Bridge load capacity was assessed at all crossings, and road width was measured at tight turns. The final solution specified: in-factory assembly → SPMT loading → roll-on/roll-off vessel loading → breakbulk ocean transport → roll-off discharge → river barge → SPMT unloading at site.

### Breakbulk Charter

Given the equipment dimensions and schedule requirements, we chartered a breakbulk vessel to carry all equipment in a single shipment, eliminating multiple handling risks. The stowage plan was professionally calculated and simulated to ensure vessel stability and cargo securing met international maritime standards.

### GPS Tracking & Project Management

Each equipment piece was fitted with GPS trackers for real-time location and status monitoring. Project managers were deployed at each key node for loading supervision, progress coordination, and issue resolution. Daily progress reports and an anomaly alert system were established.

## Results

All equipment was delivered safely and on schedule, achieving three "zeros":

- **Zero Delay**: Every piece arrived on schedule
- **Zero Incidents**: No safety accidents, no cargo damage
- **Zero Claims**: No insurance claims, no client claims

The client gave high praise at the project review meeting, designated this project as a benchmark logistics case, and signed service contracts for two subsequent projects.
